Wildfire Hazard Scoring Model

How the 1–10 hazard score is calculated — full transparency

Overview

The hazard score combines six independent data signals, each sourced from authoritative open-data APIs. Every factor is normalized to a 0–10 scale, multiplied by its weight, and summed. The result is clamped to the 1–10 range.

Scoring Formula

Composite Score

Score = (FHSZ × 0.60) + (History × 0.15) + (FFWI × 0.12) + (Slope × 0.06) + (CPUC × 0.05) + (Drought × 0.02)

Each factor returns a value in the 0–10 range before weighting. The final score is rounded to one decimal place and clamped between 1 and 10.

Factor Details

Fosberg Fire Weather Index (FFWI)

Live Weather Component

The FFWI quantifies fire potential from atmospheric conditions. Calculated from temperature (°F), relative humidity (%), and wind speed (mph).

Step 1 — Moisture damping coefficient: η = 1 − 2(m/30) + 1.5(m/30)² − 0.5(m/30)³

Step 2 — Fire potential: FFWI = η × √(1 + wind²) / 0.3002

Values above 50 indicate extreme fire weather. Fetched live from api.open-meteo.com — free, no API key required.
